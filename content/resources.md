# Breast Cancer Support Resources

*You are not alone. Here are organizations, communities, and resources to support you on your journey.*

---

## National Organizations

### American Cancer Society (ACS)
- **Website:** [cancer.org](https://www.cancer.org)
- **Helpline:** 1-800-227-2345 (24/7)
- Free lodging near treatment centers (Hope Lodge)
- Free rides to treatment (Road to Recovery)
- Online support communities

### Susan G. Komen Foundation
- **Website:** [komen.org](https://www.komen.org)
- **Helpline:** 1-877-465-6636
- Financial assistance for treatment and living expenses
- Educational resources and support
- Local affiliate in South Florida

### Breastcancer.org
- **Website:** [breastcancer.org](https://www.breastcancer.org)
- Comprehensive medical information
- Active online community forums
- Expert-reviewed articles on every stage of treatment and recovery

### Living Beyond Breast Cancer (LBBC)
- **Website:** [lbbc.org](https://www.lbbc.org)
- **Helpline:** 1-888-753-5222
- Free conferences and webinars
- Peer-to-peer support matching
- Special programs for young women and women of color

### Young Survival Coalition
- **Website:** [youngsurvival.org](https://www.youngsurvival.org)
- Resources specifically for women diagnosed under 40
- Annual conferences and local meetups
- Career, fertility, and relationship guidance

### CancerCare
- **Website:** [cancercare.org](https://www.cancercare.org)
- **Helpline:** 1-800-813-4673
- Free professional counseling (phone, online, in-person)
- Financial assistance
- Support groups

## Mental Health & Counseling

### Cancer Support Community
- **Website:** [cancersupportcommunity.org](https://www.cancersupportcommunity.org)
- Free counseling, support groups, and educational workshops
- Cancer Support Helpline: 1-888-793-9355

### SAMHSA National Helpline
- 1-800-662-4357 (free, confidential, 24/7)
- Referrals to local mental health services

### Psychology Today Therapist Finder
- [psychologytoday.com/us/therapists](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/therapists)

## For Caregivers & Loved Ones

- **Caregiver Action Network:** [caregiveraction.org](https://www.caregiveraction.org)
- **Family Caregiver Alliance:** [caregiver.org](https://www.caregiver.org)
- **CancerCare Caregiver Support:** [cancercare.org/caregivers](https://www.cancercare.org/caregivers)

### How to Support Someone After Mastectomy
- Ask "How can I help?" instead of "Let me know if you need anything"
- Offer specific help: meals, rides, childcare
- Listen more than you talk
- Don't compare their journey to someone else's
- Follow their lead on when to talk about it
