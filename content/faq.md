## About Fittings

### When should I get fitted for a breast prosthesis?
Most women are ready for a permanent prosthesis **6-8 weeks after surgery**, once swelling has subsided and incisions have healed well. However, we can provide a lightweight temporary form (sometimes called a "puff") much sooner — even before you leave the hospital — for comfort and confidence during early recovery.

### What happens during a fitting?
Your private fitting with Randi takes about **60-90 minutes**. Here's what to expect:
1. A warm conversation about your needs, lifestyle, and preferences
2. Gentle measurements
3. Trying different prosthesis shapes, sizes, and materials
4. Finding the right bras to pair with your new form
5. Looking in the mirror and feeling great

There's absolutely no rush and no pressure. This is your time.

### Do I need an appointment?
Yes, we work by appointment to ensure you get Randi's full attention in a private, comfortable setting. **[Schedule here](/#schedule)** or call us.

### Can I bring someone with me?
Absolutely! Many women bring a friend, partner, or family member for moral support. Others prefer to come alone. Whatever makes you most comfortable.

### Do you offer in-home fittings?
Yes! We provide private in-home fittings throughout the greater **West Palm Beach area**. This is especially popular with women in early recovery or those who simply prefer the comfort of home.

### How long does a fitting take?
Plan for about **60-90 minutes** for your first fitting. Follow-up fittings are usually shorter (30-45 minutes).

## About Products

### What's the difference between silicone and lightweight forms?
**Silicone prostheses** ($150-$450):
- Match the weight of natural breast tissue
- Move and feel most natural
- Best for everyday wear
- Waterproof — great for swimming
- Warm to body temperature

**Lightweight/leisure forms** ($80-$200):
- 30-50% lighter than silicone
- More comfortable for hot weather, exercise, or long days
- Great for sleep and lounging
- Ideal for women with shoulder/back concerns

Many women own both and alternate based on activity and comfort.

### How do I choose the right prosthesis?
That's what Randi is here for! She considers:
- Your body shape and size
- Your surgery type (mastectomy, lumpectomy, reconstruction)
- Your lifestyle and activities
- Your clothing preferences
- Your comfort priorities

You'll try several options and choose the one that feels right to you.

### How long does a breast prosthesis last?
With proper care, a silicone prosthesis lasts **1-2 years**. Insurance typically covers replacement on this schedule. Signs it's time for a new one:
- Visible wear, tears, or discoloration
- Changes in your body weight or shape
- The form no longer fits comfortably

### Can I swim with my prosthesis?
Yes! Silicone prostheses are **completely waterproof**. Paired with a mastectomy swimsuit (with a built-in pocket), you can swim, do water aerobics, and enjoy the beach. Rinse your form with fresh water after pool or ocean use.

### Do you carry different skin tones?
Yes. We carry prostheses in a range of skin tones to best match your natural complexion.

### What about prostheses for lumpectomy?
Absolutely. We carry **partial shapers and equalizers** that restore symmetry after lumpectomy. You deserve to feel balanced and confident.

## About Insurance & Payment

### Does insurance cover breast prostheses?
**Yes!** The Women's Health and Cancer Rights Act (WHCRA) requires most insurance plans to cover breast prostheses after mastectomy. Medicare also provides coverage. [Read our full insurance guide](/content/insurance-guide).

### What does insurance typically cover?
- **1 prosthesis per side** every 1-2 years ($300-$800 value)
- **Up to 6 mastectomy bras per year** ($45-$85 each)
- Post-surgical camisoles (varies by plan)

### Do you accept Medicare?
Yes. We are a Medicare-certified supplier. Medicare Part B covers external breast prostheses and mastectomy bras.

### Do I need a prescription?
Yes, you'll need a prescription (written order) from your doctor for insurance to cover your prosthesis and bras. We can help you get the right wording.

### What if I don't have insurance?
We work with every woman regardless of insurance status. We offer:
- Competitive self-pay pricing
- Payment plans
- Connections to financial assistance programs
- Guidance on community resources

### Do you handle the insurance paperwork?
**Yes — completely.** We verify your benefits, submit claims, handle coding, and follow up on your behalf. You typically pay only your copay or coinsurance at the time of your fitting.

## Practical Questions

### Where are you located?
We serve the **West Palm Beach, Florida** area with both in-office and in-home fittings.

### What should I wear to my fitting?
Wear whatever is comfortable. A form-fitting top (like a t-shirt) is helpful to see how your new prosthesis looks under clothing.

### How do I care for my breast prosthesis?
- Wash gently with mild soap and water
- Pat dry with a soft cloth
- Store in the protective case when not wearing
- Avoid extreme heat (don't leave in a hot car)
- Don't use lotions or powders directly on the form

### What if my body changes after my fitting?
Bodies change — from treatment, weight fluctuation, aging, or further surgery. We're here for you through all of it. Come back anytime for a re-fitting or adjustment.
