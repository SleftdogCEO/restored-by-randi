After mastectomy, your bra needs change — but that doesn't mean you have to sacrifice comfort or style. Today's mastectomy bras are beautiful, functional, and designed for real life. Here's how to find the right ones for you.

## What Makes a Mastectomy Bra Different?

The key difference is **pockets** — fabric pouches inside the cups that hold a breast form securely in place. Beyond that, mastectomy bras typically feature:

- **Wider straps** for comfort and weight distribution
- **Higher necklines** for scar coverage
- **Wider side panels** for a smooth silhouette
- **Soft, breathable materials**
- **Wire-free construction** (though some styles do include underwire for extra support)

## Types of Mastectomy Bras

### Everyday Bras
Your go-to daily wear. Look for:
- Smooth fabric that disappears under clothing
- Comfortable enough for all-day wear
- Neutral colors (nude, white, black) plus fun options
- Price: $45–$85

### Sports Bras
For walking, yoga, gym, or active days:
- Wider bands and straps for security during movement
- Moisture-wicking fabric
- Higher neckline
- Secure pockets that keep forms in place during exercise
- Price: $50–$75

### Sleep/Leisure Bras
For nighttime or lounging:
- Ultra-soft, stretchy fabric
- Light support without compression
- Pullover style (no clasps to poke you while sleeping)
- Can hold a soft fiber-fill form
- Price: $30–$55

### Special Occasion Bras
For dresses, evening wear, or when you want to feel extra:
- Lace details
- Plunging necklines (with a partial form or adhesive form)
- Strapless options (limited, but they exist)
- Price: $55–$85

## How to Find Your Size

Mastectomy bra sizing follows standard bra sizing (band + cup), but fit is even more important than usual because the form needs to sit correctly in the pocket.

### Band Size
Measure snugly around your ribcage, just under your bust. Round to the nearest even number. That's your band size.

### Cup Size
This is determined by your breast form size, which is fitted to match your remaining breast (for unilateral mastectomy) or your desired size (for bilateral).

**The most important thing:** Get professionally fitted. Sizing charts online are a starting point, but nothing replaces hands-on fitting.

## My Top Tips for Bra Shopping

1. **Buy at least 3–4 bras** to start — You need to rotate while washing. Wearing the same bra every day breaks it down fast.
2. **Get fitted in person** for your first purchase — After that, you can reorder your favorites online.
3. **Don't be afraid of color and style** — Mastectomy bras aren't the sad beige contraptions they used to be. Treat yourself!
4. **Replace every 6–12 months** — Elastic wears out, pockets stretch. And insurance covers replacement.
5. **Care for them properly** — Hand wash or lingerie bag, air dry. Never put in the dryer.

## Use Your Insurance

Most insurance plans cover **4–6 mastectomy bras per year**. Medicare specifically covers 6 per year. That's a new bra almost every other month — and you're paying for this coverage whether you use it or not.

Don't leave your benefits unused.
