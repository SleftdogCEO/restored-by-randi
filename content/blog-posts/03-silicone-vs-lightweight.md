One of the first decisions you'll make during your fitting is: silicone or lightweight? Both are excellent options, and many women end up with both for different occasions. Let me break down the differences.

## Silicone Breast Forms (Full Weight)

### What They Are
Silicone forms are made from medical-grade silicone that mimics the weight, movement, and feel of natural breast tissue. They warm to your body temperature and move naturally with your body.

### Pros
- **Most natural look and feel** — They drape like a natural breast under clothing
- **Weight balance** — The weight counterbalances your remaining breast, which can help with posture and reduce back/shoulder pain
- **Natural movement** — They move with your body when you walk, turn, or bend
- **Confidence** — Many women feel most confident in a silicone form because it looks and feels so real

### Cons
- **Heavier** — Can feel tiring during long days, hot weather, or physical activity
- **Heat retention** — Silicone holds body heat, which can feel warm in Florida summers
- **Higher price point** — $150–$450 (but covered by insurance)

### Best For
- Everyday wear
- Professional settings where you want the most natural silhouette
- Women who value the weight for balance and posture
- Special occasions

## Lightweight Breast Forms

### What They Are
Lightweight forms use a combination of silicone and lighter materials (foam backing, hollow construction) to reduce weight by 30–50% compared to standard silicone forms.

### Pros
- **Significantly lighter** — Less fatigue during long days
- **Cooler** — Better ventilation, ideal for South Florida's climate
- **More comfortable for active lifestyles** — Walking, yoga, light exercise
- **Easier on the body** — Especially for women with shoulder/back issues or post-radiation sensitivity

### Cons
- **Slightly less natural movement** — They don't have the same "swing" as full silicone
- **May not balance weight evenly** — Some women miss the counterbalance effect
- **Still require a pocket bra** — Same as silicone forms

### Best For
- Hot weather and South Florida living
- Active lifestyles
- Long workdays when comfort is priority
- Women who find full silicone too heavy
- Post-radiation sensitive skin

## Why Not Both?

Here's what I tell most of my clients: **there's no rule that says you can only have one.** Many women have:

- A **silicone form** for going out, work, or occasions where they want the most natural look
- A **lightweight form** for weekends, errands, hot days, or exercise

Insurance often covers both types. It's like having comfortable shoes and dress shoes — each serves a purpose.

## Other Options to Know About

### Partial Forms (Shapers)
For lumpectomy patients or reconstruction asymmetry. They fill in specific areas rather than replacing the entire breast shape. Price: $50–$150.

### Adhesive Forms
These attach directly to your chest wall, allowing you to go braless or wear lower-cut tops. They require special adhesive. Price: $200–$500.

### Fiber-Fill (Soft) Forms
Ultralight, washable, and perfect for sleeping, lounging, or immediately post-surgery. Price: $20–$60.

## The Best Way to Decide

Try them on. Seriously. Reading about breast forms online is helpful, but nothing compares to feeling the difference yourself. During your fitting, I'll have you try both types and you'll know immediately which one feels right.

Some women try a lightweight form and say "this is it — so comfortable." Others try silicone and say "this is the one — it feels like me." Both reactions are perfect.
