# Insurance Coverage Guide for Mastectomy Products

*Understanding your rights and getting the coverage you deserve.*

---

One of the most common questions we hear is: **"Will my insurance cover this?"** The good news is that federal law protects your right to breast prostheses and mastectomy products. Here's everything you need to know.

---

## Your Legal Rights: The Women's Health and Cancer Rights Act (WHCRA)

### What Is WHCRA?
The **Women's Health and Cancer Rights Act of 1998** is a federal law that requires group health plans and health insurance issuers that provide coverage for mastectomies to also cover:

- ✅ **Reconstruction** of the breast on which the mastectomy was performed
- ✅ **Surgery and reconstruction** of the other breast to produce a symmetrical appearance
- ✅ **Prostheses** (external breast forms)
- ✅ **Treatment of physical complications** of mastectomy, including lymphedema

### Who Is Covered?
WHCRA applies to:
- Most employer-sponsored group health plans
- Individual health insurance policies
- Plans purchased through the ACA marketplace

### What This Means for You
If your insurance covers mastectomy, they **must** also cover breast prostheses and related products. This is not optional — it's the law.

---

## What's Typically Covered

### Breast Prostheses
- **One prosthesis per side** every 1-2 years (varies by plan)
- Insurance typically covers **$300–$800 per prosthesis**
- Both silicone and lightweight forms qualify
- Custom and standard forms are covered

### Mastectomy Bras
- **Up to 6 mastectomy bras per year** with most plans
- Must be pocketed bras designed for prostheses
- Sports bras, everyday bras, and sleep bras may all qualify

### Other Covered Items (Varies by Plan)
- Post-surgical camisoles
- Mastectomy swimwear (some plans)
- Compression garments for lymphedema
- Breast form adhesives and accessories

## Medicare Coverage

### Medicare Part B Covers:
- **One external breast prosthesis per side** (every 1-2 years or when medically necessary)
- **Mastectomy bras** (up to 6 per year in many cases)
- **Custom prostheses** when medically necessary

### Important Medicare Details
- You need a **prescription (written order)** from your doctor
- The supplier (that's us!) must be **Medicare-certified**
- Medicare typically covers **80%** of the approved amount
- Your **Medigap or supplemental insurance** may cover the remaining 20%
- Prostheses are classified under **HCPCS codes L8000–L8039**

### Common Medicare Codes
| Code | Description | Typical Coverage |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| L8020 | Breast prosthesis, silicone or equal | $300–$450 |
| L8030 | Breast prosthesis, custom | $450–$800 |
| L8015 | External breast prosthesis garment (bra) | $45–$85 each |
| L8001 | Breast prosthesis, mastectomy form | $150–$300 |

## How to File an Insurance Claim

### Step 1: Get a Prescription
Ask your surgeon or primary care doctor for a written prescription for:
- "External breast prosthesis" (specify left, right, or bilateral)
- "Mastectomy bras" (specify quantity)

**Tip:** Use the medical term "breast prosthesis" — not "breast form" — on the prescription.

### Step 2: Check Your Benefits
Call the number on the back of your insurance card and ask:
- "What is my coverage for external breast prostheses?" (HCPCS codes L8000-L8039)
- "What is my coverage for mastectomy bras?" (HCPCS code L8015)
- "Do I need prior authorization?"
- "Is there a preferred supplier network?"
- "What is my out-of-pocket cost (copay/deductible)?"

### Step 3: Come See Us
Bring your:
- Insurance card
- Prescription from your doctor
- Any prior authorization number (if required)

### If a Claim Is Denied
Don't panic. Denials are common and often overturned. Common reasons and solutions:
- **"Not medically necessary"** → Your doctor can submit a letter of medical necessity
- **"Out of network"** → Ask about out-of-network benefits or single-case agreements
- **"Frequency limit exceeded"** → We can help document medical necessity for replacement
- **"Wrong code"** → We'll resubmit with the correct coding

**You have the right to appeal any denial.** We'll help you through the process.

## Tips for Maximizing Your Coverage

1. **Get your prescription early** — even before you're ready for your fitting
2. **Know your plan year** — benefits often reset on January 1st or your anniversary date
3. **Use all your bra allowance** — most plans cover 6 bras per year; don't leave benefits unused
4. **Keep receipts and records** — especially for any out-of-pocket expenses (tax-deductible as medical expenses)
5. **Ask about replacement** — if your prosthesis is worn or your body has changed, you may qualify for a new one before the standard replacement period
6. **Check for additional benefits** — some plans cover lymphedema sleeves, post-surgical garments, and more
