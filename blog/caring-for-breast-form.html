Caring for Your Breast Form: Maintenance Tips That Extend Its Life

November 26, 2025 | By ** Randi, Board Certified Mastectomy Fitter

Simple daily care to keep your prosthesis looking and feeling great for years.

Your breast prosthesis is an investment — in your comfort, your confidence, and your daily life. With simple, consistent care, a quality silicone form will last its full 2-year lifespan (and sometimes beyond). Here's how to take care of it.

Daily Care

End of Each Day

1. Remove your form from your bra — Don't leave it in the bra overnight. Both need air.

2. Wipe gently with a soft, damp cloth to remove body oils and perspiration.

3. Pat dry with a soft towel — never rub.

4. Store in its case or cradle — Most forms come with a storage container. Use it! Storing on a flat surface keeps the shape.

Morning Routine

1. Check for damage — Quick visual check for any cracks, tears, or discoloration.

2. Place gently into bra pocket — Don't force or fold the form.

Weekly Deep Clean

Once a week, give your form a proper wash:

1. Fill a basin with lukewarm water and a small amount of mild soap (like Dove or a dedicated prosthesis wash)

2. Gently wash the form with your hands — no scrubbing, no washcloths

3. Rinse thoroughly in clean lukewarm water

4. Pat dry with a soft towel

5. Air dry completely before storing — place on a clean, soft towel away from direct heat or sunlight

What NOT to Use

  • ❌ Hot water (damages silicone)
  • ❌ Harsh detergents, bleach, or alcohol
  • ❌ Abrasive scrubbers or cloths
  • ❌ Hair dryer or direct heat
  • ❌ Perfume or lotions directly on the form

Bra Care

Your mastectomy bras need love too — and proper bra care keeps the pockets in good shape for your form.

  • Hand wash in cool water with gentle detergent (best option)
  • Or machine wash in a lingerie bag on delicate/cold cycle
  • Never put in the dryer — Air dry by laying flat or hanging
  • Rotate between at least 3 bras — Elastic needs 24 hours to recover between wearings
  • Replace every 6–12 months — Stretched-out elastic and worn pockets can't support your form properly

Storage

  • Always store in the original case or cradle when not wearing
  • Keep away from heat — Never leave on a car dashboard, near a heater, or in direct sunlight
  • Don't stack heavy items on top — Pressure can deform the shape
  • Travel tip: Keep your form in its case inside your carry-on luggage (not checked bags, where temperature extremes can occur)

When to Replace Your Form

Even with perfect care, breast forms don't last forever. Signs it's time for a new one:

  • Cracks or tears in the silicone skin
  • Discoloration (yellowing or darkening)
  • Changes in shape — the form doesn't conform like it used to
  • Weight change in YOU — if you've gained or lost weight, your form may no longer match
  • It's been 2 years — Insurance covers replacement every 1–2 years for a reason

Pro Tip

When you get a new form, keep the old one! It makes a great:

  • Backup form
  • Swim form (saves your new one from chlorine)
  • Form for yard work or messy activities

Adhesive Form Care (If Applicable)

If you wear an adhesive (stick-on) breast form:

  • Clean the adhesive side after every wear with the manufacturer's recommended cleaner
  • Clean your skin before applying — no lotions, powders, or oils
  • Replace adhesive strips as recommended (varies by brand)
  • Store adhesive-side up on the included form holder

Seasonal Tips for South Florida

Living in Florida means extra consideration for heat and humidity:

  • Carry a small towel in your bag — a quick pat-down on hot days prevents moisture buildup
  • Consider a lightweight form for the hottest months
  • Use a cotton prosthesis cover — It absorbs moisture and reduces heat between the form and your skin
  • Rinse more frequently in summer — extra perspiration means extra care

A Simple Care Schedule

| When | What |

|------|------|

| Daily (evening) | Remove, wipe, dry, store in case |

| Weekly | Gentle soap wash, thorough dry |

| Monthly | Inspect for damage, check bra elastic |

| Every 6 months | Consider new bras if elastic is worn |

| Every 2 years | Schedule fitting for replacement form |

That's it. A few minutes a day keeps your form in great shape and keeps you feeling your best.
