Amoena vs American Breast Care: Comparing the Top Brands

December 17, 2025 | By ** Randi, Board Certified Mastectomy Fitter

Two leading brands, different strengths. Here's how to choose between Amoena and ABC breast forms.

When it comes to breast forms and mastectomy products, two brands come up more than any others: Amoena and American Breast Care (ABC). As a certified mastectomy fitter who has worked with both brands for over 15 years, I get asked all the time which one is better. The honest answer? Both make excellent products, but they have different strengths that matter depending on your needs.

Let me break down the Amoena vs American Breast Care comparison so you can make an informed choice.

Company Background

Amoena is a German company that's been making breast forms since the 1970s. They're known for innovation and have a huge range of products including forms, bras, swimwear, and leisure wear. Their forms are sold in over 40 countries.

American Breast Care (ABC) is a US-based company founded in 2001. They've grown quickly by focusing on comfort and natural movement. ABC is known for their ClassicTriad and Massage Form technologies that mimic the natural movement of breast tissue.

Breast Forms: Head to Head

Weight and Feel

Amoena silicone forms tend to be slightly firmer, which some women prefer because they hold their shape well under clothing. Their Natura line offers a natural weight that closely matches natural breast tissue.

ABC forms are generally known for being softer and more flexible. Their Massage Form technology allows the form to move and adapt as you move, which many women find feels more natural during everyday activities.

Shape Options

Amoena offers the widest variety of shapes I've seen. They have symmetrical forms, asymmetrical forms for left or right side, full forms, partial forms for lumpectomy, and swim forms. Their Contact line adheres directly to the chest for a secure, intimate fit.

ABC also offers a strong range of shapes. Their TriadClassic is one of the most popular forms on the market, and their asymmetrical options are excellent. ABC's SuperSoft triangle form is a favorite among my clients for casual wear.

Lightweight Options

Both brands offer lightweight versions that are 30 to 50 percent lighter than standard silicone forms.

Amoena's Natura Light line uses a blend of silicone and foam to reduce weight while maintaining a natural appearance and drape.

ABC's Lightweight series uses a similar approach. Their Lightweight Triangular form is particularly popular with active women and those in warmer climates like here in South Florida.

Adhesive Forms

Amoena's Contact series is the leader in adhesive forms. These attach directly to your chest wall using a gentle adhesive, eliminating the need for a pocketed bra. They're popular for wearing with regular bras, low-cut tops, and evening wear.

ABC offers the Flowable Back form that shapes to your body with a gel backing, though they don't have as extensive an adhesive line as Amoena.

Bras: How They Compare

Everyday Bras

Amoena's bra line is extensive and fashion-forward. They release new styles and colors regularly, and their bras tend to have a more European cut. The Hannah and Mara are perennial bestsellers.

ABC bras focus on comfort and simplicity. Their T-shirt bra and lace bra options are well-made and durable. Some clients feel ABC bras run slightly more generous in sizing.

Sports Bras

Amoena's performance sports bra is excellent, but I actually find that many of my active clients prefer the Anita Active brand specifically for sports. Between just these two brands, both offer solid athletic options.

Price Comparison

Pricing is very similar between the two brands:

  • Standard silicone forms: $250-$400 for both
  • Lightweight forms: $200-$350 for both
  • Mastectomy bras: $45-$70 for both
  • Adhesive forms: Amoena $280-$420, ABC $250-$380

Insurance typically covers products from both brands equally.

Swimwear

Amoena has a much larger swimwear collection. They release a new swim line every year with modern, fashionable designs. Their swimsuits have built-in pockets and UPF 50+ protection.

ABC offers a smaller swimwear selection but the pieces they make are well-designed and durable. If variety in swim fashion is important to you, Amoena has the edge here.

Which Should You Choose?

Based on my experience fitting thousands of women, here's my general guidance:

Choose Amoena if you want the widest variety of styles, prefer a firmer form that holds its shape, want adhesive form options, or care about having lots of swimwear choices.

Choose ABC if you prefer a softer, more flexible form, want something that moves more naturally, like a simpler product line that's easier to navigate, or prefer slightly softer bra fabrics.

Choose either if you want a lightweight form, need insurance-covered products, or want reliable quality.

The Real Answer

The best brand is the one that feels right on your body. Every woman's anatomy, surgery, and preferences are different. That's why a professional fitting matters so much. I carry both brands and many others, and I'll always recommend what actually works best for you, not what has the biggest marketing budget.
