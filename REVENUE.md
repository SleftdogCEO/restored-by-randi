## 1. Insurance Reimbursement — Primary Revenue

### This is the #1 revenue driver. Most customers have insurance that covers mastectomy products.

### HCPCS Codes & Reimbursement Rates

| HCPCS Code | Description | Typical Reimbursement | Frequency |
|------------|-------------|----------------------|-----------|
| **L8030** | Breast prosthesis, silicone or equal | $300–$500 | Every 1-2 years |
| **L8031** | Breast prosthesis, silicone or equal, with integral adhesive | $400–$600 | Every 1-2 years |
| **L8032** | Nipple prosthesis, reusable, any type | $50–$150 | As needed |
| **L8035** | Custom breast prosthesis, post mastectomy | $500–$800 | Every 2 years |
| **L8039** | Breast prosthesis, not otherwise specified | $200–$500 | Varies |
| **A4280** | Adhesive skin support attachment for breast prosthesis | $10–$30 each | As needed |

### Mastectomy Bras (Billed as part of prosthesis supply)
- Typically billed at **$50–$100 per bra**
- Most insurance covers **3–4 bras per year**
- Medicare: covers bras as prosthetic supply
- Revenue per patient per year (bras alone): **$150–$400**

### Insurance Revenue Per Customer Per Year
| Item | Low Estimate | High Estimate |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 Prosthesis | $300 | $800 |
| 3-4 Bras | $150 | $400 |
| Accessories/adhesives | $30 | $100 |
| **Total per customer/year** | **$480** | **$1,300** |

### Key Insurance Facts
- **Medicare** covers prostheses + bras with physician prescription
- **Medicaid** varies by state but most cover under DME
- **Private insurance** — Women's Health and Cancer Rights Act (WHCRA) **requires** coverage
- **Billing requires:** Licensed/certified fitter, physician prescription, proper HCPCS coding
- **Collection rate target:** 85-90% of billed amount (account for denials, adjustments)

## 10. Break-Even Analysis

### Fixed Monthly Costs
| Expense | Monthly Cost |
|---------|-------------|
| Rent (small retail space) | $1,500–$3,000 |
| Insurance (business + liability) | $200–$400 |
| Utilities | $150–$300 |
| Software (Shopify, Mailchimp, etc.) | $100–$300 |
| Phone/Internet | $100–$200 |
| Certification maintenance | $50–$100 |
| **Total Fixed** | **$2,100–$4,300** |

### Variable Costs (per sale)
| Expense | % of Revenue |
|---------|-------------|
| Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) | 35–45% |
| Payment processing | 2.5–3% |
| Shipping (online orders) | 5–8% |
| Marketing/advertising | 5–10% |
| **Total Variable** | **47.5–66%** |

### Break-Even Calculation
| Scenario | Monthly Fixed | Contribution Margin | Break-Even Revenue |
|----------|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Conservative | $4,300 | 34% (1 - 66%) | **$12,647/month** |
| Moderate | $3,200 | 42% (1 - 58%) | **$7,619/month** |
| Optimistic | $2,100 | 52.5% (1 - 47.5%) | **$4,000/month** |

### Break-Even Timeline
- **Conservative:** Month 4–6
- **Moderate:** Month 2–3
- **Optimistic:** Month 1–2

### Key Insight
Insurance reimbursement dramatically improves margins because:
1. No marketing cost to acquire (patient needs the product medically)
2. Higher reimbursement rates than retail pricing in many cases
3. Guaranteed recurring revenue (replacement cycles)
4. No price sensitivity (insurance pays, patient pays small copay)

## Revenue Optimization Strategies

### Quick Wins (Month 1-3)
1. **Maximize insurance billing** — bill every eligible item, every time
2. **Bundle products** — prosthesis + 3 bras + accessories at fitting
3. **Capture emails** — every visitor joins email list for reorder reminders
4. **Google Business Profile** — optimize for "near me" searches

### Growth Plays (Month 3-12)
1. **Cancer center partnerships** — on-site fitting days
2. **Content marketing** — blog + social driving organic traffic
3. **Referral program** — $25 credit for each referred customer
4. **Expand product line** — lymphedema, compression, post-surgical

### Scale Plays (Year 2-3)
1. **Second location** or mobile fitting van
2. **Hire additional fitters** — multiply appointment capacity
3. **Private label products** — higher margins on own-brand bras
4. **Insurance billing service** — bill for other small boutiques (fee-based)
5. **Training/certification program** — train new fitters ($500-$1,000/student)
