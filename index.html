How to Measure at Home

Finding the right fit is easy with our step-by-step guide. All you need is a soft measuring tape and a mirror.

1

Measure Your Band Size

Wrap the measuring tape snugly around your ribcage, just under your bust. Keep the tape level and breathe normally. Round to the nearest whole number — if it's even, that's your band size. If odd, add 1.

2

Measure Your Remaining Breast (If Applicable)

Measure around the fullest part of your remaining breast, keeping the tape level. This helps us match your prosthesis to your natural shape. For bilateral mastectomy, measure across your chest at the nipple line.

3

Note Your Previous Bra Size

Your pre-surgery bra size is a helpful starting point. Write down the brand, size, and whether it fit well. Different brands can vary, so this context helps us guide you.

4

Consider Your Lifestyle

Think about what you need — a full-weight silicone form for everyday wear? A lightweight form for sleeping? A swimsuit for vacation? Let us know and we'll recommend the best options.
